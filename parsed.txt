1. Introduction to Dogs
Dogs are domesticated members of the canid family and have accompanied humans for thousands
of years. The book presents dogs as companions, workers, guardians, hunters, herders, athletes,
and cultural symbols. It explores their evolution from wolf ancestors, their social behavior, pack
instincts, communication methods, and their extraordinary adaptability to human environments. The
text also discusses landrace dogs, hybrids, kennels, dog parks, and the role of canine social
structures in shaping behavior. Dogs are domesticated members of the canid family and have

working ability, movement, endurance, and breed identity. Coat varieties, grooming requirements,
and inherited traits are discussed in detail, along with controversial practices such as docking and

prevention, and treatment approaches are reviewed. The book highlights responsible ownership,

7. Dogs in History, Culture, and Society

powerful symbols of loyalty, courage, companionship, and service. Their influence extends beyond
practical work into mythology, storytelling, science, entertainment, and everyday family life. The
